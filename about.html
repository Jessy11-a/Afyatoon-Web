Nuru The Fight against FGM

Nuru, to mean light and derived from the name of a main character from a 3D animation series against female genital mutilation (FGM) is an educational and empowering tool for collective youth engagement to fight FGM. Success stories of victim and survivors from the female genital mutilation are an indicator of the influence youth have now and as future decision makers, shapers and disrupters of unfavorable society norms.

NURU is creatively making awareness on what FGM is truly, its adverse effects both short and long term with the use of story based, educative and entertaining visual arts. It is creating room for male involvement and helping the adolescent girls and young women who were victims to acquire coping skills and mechanisms for psychological trauma and give them vigilance into seeking early and proper care in light of pregnancy due to their increased risks for maternal and neonatal complications.

Nuru The Fight against FGM

This is an in-depth Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) course for journalism students and young journalists across the country, it is designed to be a three stages AMR course; Beginner knowledge on AMR, an intermediate and advance knowledge coupled with an in-depth systematic nurturing program and AMR based content dissemination among the Tanzanian community. It is the next best step to empowering and enabling Journalists; the AMR Communication frontliners to communicate effectively about AMR to the Tanzanian community. The project is based on training up to 6,000 journalism students and young journalists yearly on AMR, creative production of content in the field of AMR and effective ways to report it; to disseminate these products to up to half the Tanzania population, creating a community with AMR literacy and capable to take actions to reducing the burden on AMR in Tanzania.

Project activities

  • Human Centered Design
  • Course material production
  • Workshop and hackathons
  • Community dissemination
